Our History

A Legacy of Community, Service, and Remembrance

History of Union Beach

Union Beach, a vibrant coastal community in Monmouth County, New Jersey, has a rich history that dates back to the early 20th century. The borough was officially formed on March 16, 1925, when it separated from portions of Raritan Township, becoming an independent municipality with its own identity and governance.

The area's development was closely tied to the Jersey Shore's growing popularity as a summer destination. Early settlers were drawn to the waterfront location along Raritan Bay, and the community quickly grew from a seasonal retreat into a year-round residential area. The name "Union Beach" reflects the coming together of different communities and the unity of residents who worked to establish this distinctive shore town.

Throughout the decades, Union Beach has faced challenges with resilience, particularly during natural disasters and economic changes. The community's strong spirit and dedication to preserving its heritage have been constants through periods of growth and transformation. Today, Union Beach continues to honor its past while looking toward the future, maintaining its close-knit community character and connection to the waterfront that first drew settlers to this special place.

Union Beach Memorial Library

The Union Beach Memorial Library holds a special place in the heart of our community, serving not only as a center for learning and culture but also as a lasting tribute to local heroes. In 1946, the library was officially renamed the Union Beach Memorial Library in memory of 12 brave local men who made the ultimate sacrifice during World War II.

These 12 servicemen, who gave their lives in service to their country during one of history's most significant conflicts, came from the families and neighborhoods of Union Beach. Their courage and dedication exemplified the very best of the American spirit, and the community chose to honor their memory by dedicating the library to their legacy. This memorial designation ensures that their sacrifice will never be forgotten and that future generations will remember the price of freedom.

Over the decades, the library has evolved from a small local collection into a vital community resource. As a proud member of the Monmouth County Library System, Union Beach Memorial Library provides residents with access to an extensive collection of materials, digital resources, and programs that serve all ages. The library continues to fulfill its dual mission: providing exceptional library services while serving as a permanent memorial to those who served.

The connection to the Monmouth County library system has greatly expanded the resources available to Union Beach residents, offering access to a vast network of books, digital materials, interlibrary loan services, and shared programming. This partnership ensures that our small community library can provide world-class services while maintaining its intimate, local character and its sacred mission of remembrance.

Timeline of Significant Events

1925

Borough Formation

Union Beach officially formed as an independent borough on March 16, 1925, separating from portions of Raritan Township. This marked the beginning of Union Beach's journey as a distinct community in Monmouth County.

1941-1945

World War II Era

The community sent many of its sons and daughters to serve in World War II. Tragically, 12 local men made the ultimate sacrifice during the war, giving their lives in service to their country and the cause of freedom.

1946

Memorial Library Dedication

In honor of the 12 local men killed in World War II, the library was officially renamed the Union Beach Memorial Library. This dedication created a lasting tribute to ensure their sacrifice would never be forgotten.

1970s-1980s

Library Growth and Development

The library expanded its collections and services, growing alongside the community it served. New programs and resources were added to meet the evolving needs of residents.

1990s-2000s

Monmouth County System Integration

Union Beach Memorial Library joined the Monmouth County Library System, providing residents with access to an expanded network of resources, materials, and services while maintaining its local character and memorial mission.

2025

Centennial Milestone

Union Beach celebrates 100 years since its formation as a borough, marking a century of community growth, resilience, and service to residents.

Community Heritage & Development

The story of Union Beach is one of community resilience, waterfront heritage, and the enduring spirit of a small town with a big heart. From its founding in 1925 to the present day, Union Beach has maintained its distinctive character as a close-knit community where neighbors know each other and work together for the common good.

The community's connection to the water has shaped its identity throughout the decades. Generations of residents have enjoyed the beaches of Raritan Bay, and the waterfront continues to be central to community life. This maritime heritage connects Union Beach to the broader history of the Jersey Shore and New Jersey's coastal communities.

Through challenges including economic shifts, natural disasters, and social changes, Union Beach residents have demonstrated remarkable resilience and commitment to their community. The library has been a constant throughout these changes, providing a stable institution where residents can gather, learn, and connect with their shared history.

Today, as Union Beach looks to the future, the community remains committed to honoring its past, celebrating its heroes, and building on the strong foundation established by earlier generations. The Union Beach Memorial Library stands as a symbol of this commitment—a place where history is preserved, the fallen are remembered, and the community comes together.
